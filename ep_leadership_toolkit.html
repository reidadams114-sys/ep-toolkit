Standard 1:1 Agenda Structure
Use this as your default meeting flow
0–5m
Personal Check-in
How are they really doing? Not a status update — a human moment. One genuine question.
5–20m
Their Agenda First
What do they most need from you today? Surface blockers, decisions, or support needs they're carrying.
20–35m
Project & Priority Pulse
Quick status on 1–2 active initiatives. Not a full report — alignment and flag-raising only.
35–45m
Your Agenda + Coaching
What you need from them + one development observation or encouragement. End with clarity on next steps.
45–50m
Confirm Action Items
Name the 1–3 things each of you owns before next meeting. Keep it concrete.

Framework
The 9-Month Horizon Map
How to think about each phase of your planning window
NOW
M1–2
Stabilize & Diagnose
Finance HR Ministry
What's broken or fragile right now? Where are dependencies that could fail? Get honest data on capacity across all three areas. These 60 days are about clear-eyed diagnosis, not action.
NEAR
M3–4
Align & Decide
Key Hires Budget Structure
Make the structural decisions now that take 3–6 months to play out. Hiring, budget reallocation, team restructures. Decisions delayed here arrive as crises in months 7–9.
MID
M5–6
Build & Equip
Onboarding Systems Training
New hires are ramping. New systems are being adopted. This phase feels slow — resist the urge to rush it. Your job here is removing obstacles and holding the vision steady while others execute.
LONG
M7–8
Execute & Measure
OKRs Review Adjust
Plans are running. Review against your leading indicators. Make the minor corrections now that keep you from major pivots later. This is high-trust delegation territory.
END
M9
Arrive & Reset
Evaluate Celebrate Next Horizon
You're here — did you arrive where you planned? Run a structured retrospective across Finance, HR, and Ministry. Then immediately begin the next 9-month planning cycle. The horizon is always moving.
📊
Leading Indicators to Track Monthly
What to watch now to forecast where you'll be in 9 months
Finance
  • Reserve fund trend (3-mo rolling)
  • Giving vs. budget variance
  • Ministry spend by % of budget used
  • Upcoming major capital decisions
HR / People
  • Open positions & time-to-fill
  • Staff capacity ratings (self-reported)
  • Attrition risk signals
  • Succession gaps identified
Ministry
  • Attendance trend (not just Sunday)
  • Volunteer engagement rate
  • First-time guest return rate
  • Small group / connection %

Morning Affirmations
Read daily — slow down on the one that grabs you
Jesus is first in my life. I exist to serve and glorify Him.
I love my wife (Ephesians 5:25) and will lay down my life to serve her.
I love people (Romans 12:10) and believe the best about others even when inclined to do otherwise.
I am disciplined. (1 Thessalonians 4:4) Christ in me is stronger (Romans 7:24-25) than the wrong desires in me.
I am growing closer to Jesus (2 Peter 3:18) every day. Because of Christ, my family is closer, my body is stronger, my faith is deeper, and my leadership is sharper.
I am anointed, empowered, equipped, and called (Matthew 28:19-20) to reach people far from God.
I am creative, innovative, driven, focused, and blessed beyond measure (Ephesians 1:3) because the Holy Spirit is inside (2 Timothy 1:14) of me.
I develop leaders. That's not something I do. It's who I am. (1 Peter 4:10)
I wake up with purpose (Romans 8:28), direction, and meaning every day of my life.
Pain is my friend. I rejoice in suffering (1 Peter 4:13) because Christ suffered for me.
I bring my best (2 Timothy 2:15) and then some. It's what I bring after I do my best that makes the difference.
The world will be different and better because I served Jesus (2 Corinthians 5:18-19) today.
I am strong and mighty. I have the same power (Ephesians 1:18-20) that raised Christ from the dead living inside of me.
I am a weapon of righteousness and goodness (2 Corinthians 6:7) in a world of darkness.
I am not a slave to my habits. I am not a prisoner to an addiction. I have been rescued (Colossians 1:13-14) from the power of darkness and brought into the kingdom of God's light.
I am not my past. I am not what I did. I am who God says I am — forgiven (Ephesians 1:7), redeemed, and free (Galatians 5:1).
Worry is not my master. I trust in God (John 14:1). His peace guards (Philippians 4:6-7) my heart, mind, and soul in Christ Jesus.
I have the mind of Jesus Christ (1 Corinthians 2:16) directing my thoughts. I have the Word of God guiding (Psalms 119:105) my steps.
My God will bless me abundantly, so that in all things, at all times, having all I need (2 Peter 1:3) I will abound (2 Corinthians 9:8) in every good work.

My Leadership Manifesto
Slow down on the one that grabs you.
🎯
Leadership Priorities
  • Define success clearly
  • The right people in the right seat
  • Establish irreducible simplicity and excellence
  • Mobilize people to reach their ones
  • Communicate constantly
  • Apply constant. gentle. pressure
  • Stay humble and hungry
🧭
Core Strategy
  • Develop a culture that equips our people to reach their ONES
  • Narrow our focus to multiply our impact
  • Build neighborhood small groups that lay foundations for campuses
  • Move people from visitor to volunteer as fast as possible
  • Become the best in the world at follow up and next steps
📐
Pain + Reflection = Progress
An accurate understanding of reality is the essential foundation for producing good outcomes.
  • Have clear goals
  • Identify and don't tolerate problems that stand in the way of goals
  • Accurately diagnose those problems
  • Design plans that explicitly lay out tasks to get around your problems
  • Implement these plans — do the tasks
🏆
Pillars of a Killer Team
  • Foster togetherness — 10M resigned; primary reasons: leaders don't know them & we don't celebrate wins
  • Recruit and keep the best talent
  • Bolster accountability
  • Structure for growth and peace
  • Maintain rhythms and finish lines
✨
Principles of Excellence
  • The aggregation of marginal gains — 1% improvement in everything we do
  • Excellence is not about radical changes, but accruing small improvements over time
  • Time will magnify whatever you feed it
  • If you are struggling to improve, the problem isn't you — it's your systems
  • You do not rise to the level of your goals; you fall to the level of your systems
  • The more things we chase, the slower progress will be
👥
Hiring Philosophy
Hunt for: passion to develop others · obsessed with outcomes · humble and hungry
  • Work for what they want, not what others want of them
  • Stress-test opinions by having the smartest people challenge them
  • Wary of overconfidence — good at not knowing
  • Wrestle with reality; reflect on results to improve
  • 2 out of every 3 hires should be from the outside
"It's the whelming candidate you must avoid at all costs — they infuse an organization with mediocrity." — Danny Meyer
💡
Leadership Learnings
A weekly read-through — slow down on the one that grabs you
  • "The one with the most hope in the room has the greatest authority."
  • Think critically, but don't become critical.
  • Wisdom is the ability to apply knowledge.
  • Walk around with a towel, not a clipboard.
  • You don't keep people smarter than you by being smarter than them — you do it by loving and caring for them.
  • Change is inevitable. People don't fight change — they fight loss.
  • You cannot be emotionally immature and spiritually mature. A sign of emotional maturity: the time between my first response and my best response is getting shorter.
  • What if sometimes disappointing people is just a natural part of living out our calling?
  • How will I feel about this in 10 minutes? 10 weeks? 10 months?
  • Vision leaks every 6 weeks.
  • The opposite of conflict is not peace — it's artificial harmony.
  • A well-led organization cannot survive bad management. Management will be the lid on your mission.
  • You impress at a distance, but you impact at short-range.
  • Performers see under-performers. They wonder if you have the EQ to see it and the courage to confront it.
  • Consistency is the standard. Perfection is difficult to deliver consistently.
  • Define run-hard periods and cooldown periods. Rhythm is crucial for longevity.
  • Am I living like I believe I am enough?

Leading While Grieving
  • Acknowledge the loss
  • Accept the gift of mourning
  • Adjust your expectations in seasons of loss
  • Embrace the waves of grief and productivity
  • Replenishment cycle: Spiritual · Physical · Intellectual · Emotional · Social

How This Works
